# Camp & Ecology — the "what do we do" arc

*Captured 2026-07-18 from Blaine's playtest message, for co-design. The
first quest (below) ships now; everything after is sketched for reaction.*

Blaine's words, distilled: build a real camp from gathered materials — fire,
a place to sleep. Eventually food: harvest, plant crops near home that feed
you. A harvest UI. Maybe you *don't* know ahead of time what's edible.
Surplus harvest attracts critters. The whole island should be a dependent
ecology you discover — prey and predators, Scavenger's Reign: "is this
thing going to hurt me? can I eat it? what does it depend on?" Biomes that
feel totally different; maybe scary; some critters dangerous; craft things
to protect yourself or make yourself non-threatening. Eventually witness
creatures adapting. Survival × Stardew, **without being tedious**.

## Pillar tension, named early

"Peaceful" and "maybe it's scary" can coexist the Scavenger's Reign way:
**danger as respect, not combat**. Proposal: nothing in Wander kills you or
takes your things. Danger = being warned, chased off, stung (screen shake,
a forced retreat, a lingering wariness the critter remembers). Protection
crafting = becoming *ignorable* (scent masks, quiet shoes) more than armor.
Hunger, if it comes, is a **mood system, not a death timer**: an unfed
wanderer walks slower at night, murmurs turn hungrier, critters notice.
Nothing to lose; much to want. ← needs Blaine's yes/no.

## Arc 1 — the camp (first quest; light pass ships now)

- Materials with obvious sources: **driftwood** washed onto beaches,
  **loose stones** at the rock's edge. F gathers them like seeds.
- **Campfire** at the garden home: 4 driftwood + 3 stones, press H beside
  your home. Burns forever at night: flame, sparks, warm light circle.
- Next steps (not yet built): a **bedroll** (sleep to skip to dawn — the
  first time control verb), a **windbreak/lean-to** that grows the camp
  visually, a **rack** for drying harvest.

## Arc 2 — harvest & food (needs co-design)

- Some plants carry **fruit/tubers** (a genome trait: `yield`). Harvest
  with F on mature plants; a small basket UI when several things are in
  reach (extends the inspect panel rather than adding a new screen).
- **Edibility is discovered, not labeled**: taste a little (nibble verb) →
  journal records "agreed with you" / "never again" per species. Genome
  affects it (bitter = high sat + low glow? co-design the mapping).
- Crops near home: sown food plants in the garden bed grow faster (already
  true) — add **replanting from harvest** so a food garden sustains itself.
- **Surplus attracts life**: harvested-but-unclaimed food on the ground
  draws critters (they already have favorite species); a fed critter
  lingers near camp. This is the trust system's front door.

## Arc 3 — the dependent ecology (the big one)

- A real (small) food web per island: plants → herbivore critters →
  **one predator species** (new). Predators hunt critters, not you — but
  guard their kills and bluff-charge anything close (the "scary").
- Every species' journal page grows a **"depends on"** line discovered by
  watching: see a critter eat a plant → the link is recorded. Watch enough
  and the island's web draws itself in the journal.
- Biome identity: each habitat gets its own critter + predator + flora
  triad so a marsh *feels* nothing like a beach (partly true already).
- Adaptation witnessed: critters already have per-species genomes; add
  drift + selection pressure (prey near predators gets faster/warier over
  island-days) so long-lived islands visibly change behavior.
